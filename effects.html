Side Effects and Precautions

Understanding the potential side effects and precautions associated with medications is essential for safe usage. Below are common side effects and precautions for some frequently used medications:

Aspirin

Side Effects: Nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, heartburn, and prolonged bleeding.

Precautions: Avoid if you have a history of stomach ulcers or gastrointestinal bleeding. Consult a doctor before use if pregnant or breastfeeding.

Ibuprofen

Side Effects: Stomach pain, dizziness, headache, rash, and increased blood pressure.

Precautions: Use with caution if you have kidney issues, heart disease, or gastrointestinal problems. Not recommended during pregnancy, especially in the third trimester.

Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

Side Effects: Allergic reactions, nausea, and liver damage in cases of overdose.

Precautions: Do not exceed recommended doses; avoid alcohol while taking. Consult a doctor if you have liver disease.

Antihistamines (e.g., Diphenhydramine)

Side Effects: Drowsiness, dry mouth, dizziness, and difficulty urinating.

Precautions: Avoid alcohol and driving after taking. Not recommended for elderly patients without a doctor's advice.

Metformin

Side Effects: Gastrointestinal upset, metallic taste, and risk of lactic acidosis (rare).

Precautions: Inform your doctor if you have kidney issues or plan to undergo surgery. Monitor blood sugar levels regularly.

For any concerns regarding side effects or precautions, please consult your healthcare provider.
