Dosage Guide

Proper dosage is crucial for the effectiveness of medication and to minimize side effects. Below is a general dosage guide for some common medications. Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any medication.

Aspirin

Indication: Pain relief, anti-inflammatory.

Dosage: For adults, the typical dose is 325 mg to 650 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed, not exceeding 4,000 mg in a 24-hour period.

Notes: Do not give to children under 12 unless directed by a doctor.

Ibuprofen

Indication: Pain relief, fever reduction.

Dosage: For adults, take 200 mg to 400 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed, not exceeding 1,200 mg per day unless directed by a physician.

Notes: Take with food or milk to reduce stomach upset.

Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

Indication: Pain relief, fever reducer.

Dosage: For adults, the usual dose is 500 mg to 1,000 mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed, not exceeding 3,000 mg per day.

Notes: Overdose can cause liver damage; avoid alcohol while taking.

Antihistamines (e.g., Diphenhydramine)

Indication: Allergies, motion sickness.

Dosage: For adults, the usual dose is 25 mg to 50 mg every 4 to 6 hours, not exceeding 300 mg per day.

Notes: May cause drowsiness; avoid operating heavy machinery.

Metformin

Indication: Type 2 diabetes management.

Dosage: For adults, the usual starting dose is 500 mg to 1,000 mg daily, gradually increasing based on tolerance and blood sugar levels.

Notes: Take with meals to minimize gastrointestinal side effects.

If you have any questions or concerns about your medication, please consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
